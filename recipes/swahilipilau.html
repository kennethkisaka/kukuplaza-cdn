SWAHILI CHICKEN PILAU

Chicken Pilau is a traditional East African dish, rich in spices and flavor. The dish combines chicken, rice, and spices, often cooked in a single pot for a deliciously aromatic meal.

For Chicken Marinade:

  • 500g chicken (with bones or boneless)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons oil

For Pilau Rice:

  • 2 cups basmati rice (soaked for 30 minutes)
  • 4 cups water or chicken stock
  • 2 large onions (sliced)
  • 4 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon pilau masala
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 5-6 cardamom pods
  • 5-6 whole cloves
  • 2-3 potatoes (peeled and cubed)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, mix the ginger-garlic paste, cumin, coriander, garam masala, lemon juice, oil, and salt. Add the chicken and marinate for at least 1 hour.
  2. Cook the Chicken: Heat oil in a pot and brown the marinated chicken. Once browned, remove and set aside.
  3. Prepare the Pilau Base: In the same pot, add more oil if needed and saut√© the onions until golden brown. Add minced garlic, pilau masala, black pepper, cinnamon sticks, cardamom pods, and cloves. Fry for a few minutes.
  4. Add the Rice: Add the soaked rice and cubed potatoes into the pot. Stir well to coat the rice in the spices. Pour in water or chicken stock, bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low.
  5. Cook the Pilau: Add the browned chicken back into the pot. Cover and simmer on low heat until the rice and potatoes are fully cooked (about 20-25 minutes). Do not stir to avoid breaking the rice.
  6. Serve: Once cooked, fluff the rice with a fork. Serve the pilau with kachumbari (tomato-onion salad) or yogurt on the side.

Enjoy your delicious Swahili Chicken Pilau!
