CHICKEN BIRYANI

Biryani is a mixed rice dish, mainly popular in South Asia. It is made with rice, some type of meat and spices. To cater to vegetarians, in some cases, it is prepared by substituting vegetables or paneer for the meat. Sometimes eggs and/or potatoes are also added.

For Chicken Marinade:

  • 500g chicken (with bones or boneless)
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons oil

For Rice:

  • 2 cups basmati rice (soaked for 30 minutes)
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste

For Biryani Masala:

  • 2 large onions (thinly sliced)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 2-3 green chilies (slit)
  • 1/2 cup chopped cilantro (coriander leaves)
  • 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon biryani masala (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • Saffron strands soaked in 2 tablespoons of warm milk (or 1/4 teaspoon turmeric in water as a substitute)

For Layering:

  • 1/4 cup fried onions (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons ghee
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a large bowl, mix the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, lemon juice, and salt. Add the chicken pieces to this mixture and marinate for at least 1 hour (or overnight for better flavor).
  2. Cook the Rice: Boil 4 cups of water in a large pot. Add bay leaves, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, and salt. Once the water starts boiling, add the soaked basmati rice and cook until the rice is about 70-80% cooked (it should still be firm). Drain the rice and set it aside.
  3. Prepare the Biryani Masala: In a large pan, heat ghee or oil. Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle. Add sliced onions and fry until golden brown. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft and the oil starts separating. Add slit green chilies, cilantro, mint leaves, biryani masala, and garam masala. Stir well. Add the marinated chicken and cook until the chicken is cooked through and the gravy thickens. This may take 15-20 minutes.
  4. Layering the Biryani: In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, start layering the chicken and rice. First, spread a layer of cooked chicken at the bottom, then add a layer of rice. Sprinkle some saffron milk (or turmeric water), chopped cilantro, and fried onions over the rice. Repeat the layers until all the chicken and rice are used. Finish with a final layer of rice on top and drizzle some ghee and saffron milk.
  5. Dum Cooking: Cover the pot tightly with a lid or foil to trap the steam. You can seal the edges with dough for better results. Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend (this is called dum cooking). Alternatively, you can bake the biryani at 180°C (350°F) for 20-25 minutes.
  6. Serve: Once done, gently fluff the biryani with a fork, mixing the layers. Garnish with boiled eggs and more fried onions if desired. Serve with raita (yogurt sauce) or salad on the side.

Enjoy your delicious homemade Chicken Biryani!
